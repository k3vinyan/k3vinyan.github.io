Partner in Crime

Posted on May 10, 2014

Pairing:

What was it like to pair with others to solve challenges?

At first, it was pretty intimidating because I was unsure in my own ability in coding and didn’t want to come off like I didn’t know anything. But pretty quickly I learn that we’re all in the same boat. We both are trying to develop a new skills set and even though sometime we at a whole different level when I comes to actual knowledge; I found that the more knowledgeable person is always willing to teach the other person. Everyone here to learn and develop through the program and through each other.

Was something particularly fun or rewarding?

Honestly, everything we are learning is fun/rewarding but stressful sometimes. I great when we get a solutions right out of the gate but when we don't....well, it can be very frustrating.But if I had to choose, I would choose the HTML/CSS challenge since we get to create and development our own website to our likling.

Was something frustrating and difficult for you?

EVERYTHING!!!

Feedback

How did you feel when you read your feedback?

I feel pretty comfortable with reading the feedback. Nothing has come back so far with any real negative criticism. Lets keep our finger cross.

Was the feedback you received helpful to your learning?

They are very helpful as they point out want I need to improve on and how I should perform during the next paring session.

Based on the feedback you've received, what are you going to do to improve next time you pair?

Definitely! I find myself talking out loud more and speaking my mind instead of usually taking the backseat in the situation.

How was it to write feedback?

It pretty easy. To be honest, it getting really repititive since I haven't really ran into someone who I thought was not holding their weight in the session when we pair up. I probably need to learn to be more critical of others so I can tell them how they can improve. I guess I need to work on that more.

What was most difficult for you when writing feedback for your pair?

The most difficult part of writing feedback is actually giving them some critical feedbacks. I find this hard because in most session your sort of focusing on yourself and how you interact with your pair and how you can help on finding the solution. I don't really look to find any mistake or how they can improve.

Overall, what do you think of using pairing and feedback to guide your learning?

I think how things are now is pretty helpful in my development.
